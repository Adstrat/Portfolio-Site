Biography

Born in Brighton, England, I've tried my hand at a few things. With an interest in the mind, I made a diploma in hypnotherapy in 2003, which led me to explore meditation and yoga. Some years later I pursued a yearning to make things out of wood, to build in a very physical way, and studied carpentry. Throughout this time I worked in social care, mainly with people with autism and learning disabilities.

A few years ago I moved to Berlin with my family and decided to pursue a career in web development, out of a desire to be involved in helping to steer technology in a beneficial direction.

I've always enjoyed creating, in a structured kind of way. Problem solving, making things fit, or work well, is somehow rewarding. I like that sense of completion, when things come together, to flow beautifully.

I think in this modern, technological world, there's a chance to make an impact. The internet is a mega powerful tool, which can be harnessed for great things. It is my hope that I can be a part of that.

Aside from programming, I also enjoy juggling, painting and being in nature.

In this app, users can search for events by inputing different cities and choosing the number of events to be displayed.

The start of this project was my biggest challenge, setting up AWS Lambda and writing the authentication code. I got stuck with a bug for a while which I discovered to be a simple indentation that I'd missed. But as soon as the application was up, I enjoyed creating the different features.

I still prefer to make features first and then think about the test scenarios after, but I'm slowly getting my head around how to think through the tests first.

There were times when I would hit a problem, trying to get something to display a certain way, but there's nothing more satisfying than resolving it. And sometimes discovering a neat trick or a way to do things that was better than my original idea. There's always lots of cool stuff to learn!

For this project I feel the charts and graphs are not overly useful from a UX point of view, but they are more of a showcase to practice and display different things that I can develop.
